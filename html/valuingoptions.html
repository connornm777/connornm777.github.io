Valuing Options

I found the arguments for option pricing using Black-Scholes or other models conceptually complicated and not well founded in the general case. I want to lay out simple reasoning that can capture broader phenomena, such as qualitative information about markets that can be captured in a subjective Bayesian way, and reduces to the usual formulas when the assumptions are made explicitly.

Consider a call option on an asset with current price $$S$$, a strike price of $$S_0$$, and expiration in time $$t$$, and no time value of money (or put the risk free rate at zero, updating this to include a non-zero risk free rate is relatively simple).

$$ V_c(S, S_0, t) = \int_{S_0}^{\infty} dS^{\prime} P_t(S^{\prime} | S)(S^{\prime}-S_0) $$
